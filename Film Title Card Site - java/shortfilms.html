'Celestial Powerfist - On The Wings of Destiny' 2010
Further Details

A2-Level Media Project, a power metal music video with music from the Salisbury metal group 'Riot Act'.
A truly iconic work of art that recieved the highest of marks. Matthew Pitcairn mimes the vocals and plays the part of the Hero, whilst a rarely-beardless William Bazire (in his break out role) plays the villian. Lawrence Hendry and Ruth Websdale on guitars, and Alistair Costley-White as the drummer and owner of the song.





'Celestial Powerfist - On The Wings of Destiny REDUX' 2012
Further Details

First year University project, a remake of 'Celestial Powerfist - On The Wings of Destiny'.
This time the Hero of the video is played by Mathew Laws, who also mimes the guitar. William Bazire returns as the villian, this time with beard (a look that quickly becomes part of his arsenal). Chris Taylor mimes the guitar and vocals of the track, whilst Cassandra Dalby performs the drum track.





'Placebo' 2012
Further Details

End of year one University Project, a man who recently lost his wife in a car accident tries a new research drug that allows him to spend more time with her.
Kieran Rowan, Alina Nae and Emma Saunders star in this romantic tale of love lost to death and the inabilty to move on.





'Cadaver Cuisine' 2013
Further Details

Cadaver Cuisine - And Other Tales of Bloodshed and Butchery, a two-part anthology horror that make up Matthew's film-making Diploma. Starring William Bazire in his most famous role to date, the ever insatiable 'Food Critic'. David Armah-Agyeman joined the production as 'The Chef,' also his most famous role.
When a gluttonous Food Critic scolds The Chef's signature dish, he is offered the meal of a lifetime, the hard-to-stomach Cadaver Cuisine.
Written, Directed, Shot and Edited by Matthew Pitcairn, Music by Kevin Macleod.





'Aled and The Mystic' 2013
Further Details

Aled and The Mystic - Another Tale of Bloodshed and Butchery, part-two of the anthology horror that make up Matthew's film-making diploma. Debut role for Aled Evans, also his only role to date. With David Armah-Agyeman returning as the titular 'The Mystic'. Aled causes a bit of a headache when he attempts to buy recreational drugs from a mystic who doesn't seem 'all there'.
Written, Directed, Shot and Edited by Matthew Pitcairn, Music by Kevin Macleod.





Unreleased Short Film 'Romance is Dead' Teaser Trailer
Further Details

Teaser trailer for the unreleased short film 'Romance is Dead'. With William Bazire as the 'protagonist-turn-antagonistic' Trevor, a once formerly licensed Doctor now back-alley surgeon. A romantic at heart, who after years of loneliness in a dehumanizing trade have plunged him into a dark insanity. Believing a young patient on the brink of death to be his soul mate, Trevor is about to succumb to shocking levels of perversion.
Starring Liam McAteer in his debut performance as 'Kurt,' a trash-heap of a human who crosses paths with Trevor when his girlfriend is on the brink of death.
Written, Directed, Shot and Edited by Matthew Pitcairn. Trailer Music by Billy Ocean.
